## Email Specifications

### Structure Requirements
- **Subject Line**: 6-10 words, benefit-focused or clear topic
- **Length**: 75-150 words (brief communication) OR 100-175 words (detailed explanation)
- **Format**: Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences each), scannable
- **Tone**: Professional but approachable (adjust based on audience relationship)

### Email Types

**Outreach/Introduction** (100-150 words):
- Subject: Clear value proposition or connection point
- Opening: Specific observation or mutual connection (1-2 sentences)
- Value: What you offer and why it matters (2-3 sentences)
- Proof: Brief evidence of capability or credibility
- CTA: Clear, simple ask (meeting, call, response)

**Follow-Up** (75-100 words):
- Subject: Reference previous interaction
- Opening: Acknowledge last contact
- Value reminder: Quick recap of benefit or next step
- New information: Add something useful (not just "checking in")
- CTA: Specific next step

**Announcement** (125-175 words):
- Subject: Clear news or update
- Opening: State the news immediately
- Context: Why it matters to reader
- Details: Key information in bullets (3-5 items)
- CTA: What they should do next

**Update/Status** (75-125 words):
- Subject: Clear status indicator
- Opening: Current state/progress
- Key points: Bullets for accomplishments or issues
- Next steps: What's coming or what's needed
- CTA: Action required or FYI

### Content Elements
- **Hook**: Engage reader in first 1-2 sentences
- **Clarity**: One main message per email
- **Brevity**: Respect reader's time
- **Action**: Clear next step (even if it's just "FYI")

## Prompt for Email Creation

Before creating the email, gather this information:

1. **Email Type**: Outreach, Follow-up, Announcement, Update, or Other?

2. **Recipient**:
   - Who are they? (Title, relationship to you)
   - What's your history with them? (First contact, ongoing relationship, colleague)

3. **Purpose**: What do you want to accomplish with this email?

4. **Key Message**: What's the main point in one sentence?

5. **Tone Preference**:
   - Professional Formal (C-suite, external stakeholders)
   - Professional Friendly (colleagues, warm business relationships)
   - Direct and Brief (busy executives, time-sensitive)

6. **Context/Background**: Any specific details I should know?

7. **Call-to-Action**: What should they do after reading? (Reply, schedule meeting, review document, FYI only)

8. **Length Preference**: Brief (under 100 words) or Detailed (100-175 words)?

**Context**: First contact with potential partner

**Subject**: Collaboration Opportunity: AI Integration

**Body**:
```
Hi [Name],

I noticed your recent work on enterprise AI deployment at [Company]. Your
approach to scalable implementations aligns with what we're building at Griffith.

We've successfully deployed AI solutions across 40+ engineering teams, reducing
onboarding time by 35%. I think there's an opportunity to collaborate on
[specific area].

Would you be open to a 20-minute call next week? I'll share specific ideas on
how we could work together.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Why it works**: Specific observation, concrete results, clear value, simple ask
