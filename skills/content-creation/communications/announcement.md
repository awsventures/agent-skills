## Announcement Specifications

### Structure Requirements
- **Subject/Title**: Clear, specific announcement in 6-10 words
- **Length**: 150-250 words (brief announcement) OR 250-400 words (detailed announcement)
- **Format**:
  - Headline/subject
  - Lead paragraph (what's being announced)
  - Details and context
  - Impact or benefits
  - Next steps or call-to-action
- **Tone**: Positive and informative (adjust based on news type)

### Announcement Types

**Organizational Change** (250-350 words):
- What's changing (new structure, leadership, process)
- Why the change is happening
- When it takes effect
- Who's affected
- What changes for them
- How to get more information

**New Initiative/Program** (200-300 words):
- What's being launched
- Why it matters
- Key features or benefits
- Who can participate/benefit
- When it starts
- How to get involved

**Achievement/Milestone** (150-250 words):
- What was accomplished
- Why it's significant
- Who contributed
- Impact or results
- What's next
- Recognition/thank you

**Policy or Procedure** (200-300 words):
- What's new or changing
- Effective date
- Rationale
- Key details (bullets)
- What people need to do
- Resources or support available

### Content Elements
- **Lead**: Most important information first
- **Context**: Enough background to understand significance
- **Details**: Specific information people need to know
- **Action**: Clear next steps or how to learn more
- **Tone**: Match the news (celebratory, informative, or serious)

## Prompt for Announcement Creation

Before creating the announcement, gather this information:

1. **Announcement Type**: Organizational change, new initiative, achievement, policy, or other?

2. **Audience**:
   - Who needs to know? (All staff, specific department, stakeholders)
   - What's their relationship to the news?

3. **Main News**: What are you announcing in one sentence?

4. **Timing**:
   - When does this take effect or happen?
   - When should this be communicated?

5. **Context**: Why is this happening or why does it matter?

6. **Key Details**: What are the 3-5 most important facts people need to know?

7. **Impact**: How does this affect the audience?

8. **Next Steps**: What should readers do with this information?

9. **Tone Preference**:
   - Celebratory and energizing (positive news)
   - Professional and straightforward (neutral news)
   - Serious and supportive (challenging news)

10. **Additional Information**: Where can people learn more or ask questions?

This investment in your growth strengthens our capabilities while supporting your
career development.

Contact the learning and development team.

Looking forward to seeing how you use this opportunity.
