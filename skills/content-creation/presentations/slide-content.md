## Slide Content Specifications

### Structure Requirements
- **Slide Titles**: 3-10 words, active voice, specific
- **Body Text**: Minimal - bullets or key phrases only
- **Slide Count**: Based on presentation length (see below)
- **Format**:
  - Title slide
  - Agenda/overview (optional for <10 slides)
  - Content slides
  - Summary/conclusion
  - Next steps or call-to-action
- **Tone**: Clear and confident

### Presentation Types

**Executive Update** (5-10 slides, 10-15 minutes):
- Slide 1: Title and context
- Slide 2: Key message or status
- Slides 3-4: Supporting data (metrics, accomplishments)
- Slide 5: Issues or risks
- Slide 6: Decisions needed or next steps
- Slides 7-8: Appendix (detailed data)

**Project Proposal** (10-15 slides, 20-30 minutes):
- Slide 1: Title and presenter
- Slide 2: Problem statement
- Slide 3: Proposed solution (high level)
- Slides 4-6: How it works (details)
- Slides 7-8: Benefits and ROI
- Slide 9: Timeline and milestones
- Slide 10: Budget and resources
- Slide 11: Risks and mitigation
- Slide 12: Next steps
- Slides 13-15: Appendix (technical details)

**Technical Deep Dive** (15-25 slides, 45-60 minutes):
- Slide 1: Title and agenda
- Slides 2-3: Context and background
- Slides 4-8: Architecture or design
- Slides 9-12: Implementation details
- Slides 13-16: Challenges and solutions
- Slides 17-19: Results or performance
- Slide 20: Lessons learned
- Slide 21: Next steps
- Slides 22-25: Appendix

**Training Presentation** (20-40 slides, 60-90 minutes):
- Slide 1: Title and learning objectives
- Slide 2: Agenda
- Slides 3-5: Conceptual overview
- Slides 6-15: Core content (broken into topics)
- Slides 16-20: Examples and demos
- Slides 21-25: Hands-on exercises
- Slide 26: Summary and key takeaways
- Slide 27: Resources and next steps
- Slides 28-40: Reference materials

### Slide Design Principles
- **One Idea Per Slide**: Don't cram multiple concepts
- **Minimal Text**: 3-5 bullets max, 6-8 words per bullet
- **Visual Hierarchy**: Most important information largest
- **Data Visualization**: Charts over tables when possible
- **Consistency**: Same font, colors, layout throughout
- **Readable**: Large font (24pt+ for body, 36pt+ for titles)

## Prompt for Slide Content Creation

Before creating slide content, gather this information:

1. **Presentation Type**: Executive Update, Proposal, Deep Dive, Training, or Other?

2. **Audience**:
   - Who will attend? (Executives, managers, technical team, mixed)
   - What's their familiarity with the topic?
   - What decisions do they need to make?

3. **Time Allotted**: How long is the presentation?

4. **Main Message**: What's the key takeaway in one sentence?

5. **Objective**: What should happen after this presentation?
   - Decision or approval needed
   - Information sharing only
   - Training or skill development
   - Problem solving or brainstorming

6. **Key Points**: What are the 3-5 main points to cover?

7. **Data Available**: What metrics, charts, or examples will you include?

8. **Format Preference**:
   - Mostly bullets (traditional)
   - Mostly visuals (modern, minimal text)
   - Mix of both

9. **Constraints**: Any specific requirements or limitations?

## Output Format

I will provide:

1. **Slide Outline** (numbered slides with titles)
2. **Detailed Slide Content** for each slide:
   - Slide title
   - Bullet points or key phrases
   - Notes on visuals (charts, diagrams, images)
   - Speaker notes reference (if needed)
3. **Design recommendations** (layout, visual emphasis)
4. **Timing guidance** (minutes per slide)

## Quality Checks Applied

Before delivering, I verify:
- ✓ One main idea per slide
- ✓ Slide titles are specific and descriptive
- ✓ Bullets are concise (6-8 words max)
- ✓ No more than 5 bullets per slide
- ✓ Data visualized appropriately
- ✓ Logical flow between slides
- ✓ Clear call-to-action or next steps
- ✓ Appendix for detailed data
- ✓ Consistent structure throughout
- ✓ Slide count matches time allotted (2-3 min per slide)
