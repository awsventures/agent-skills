## STYLE (Lines 18-23)

- **Use active voice** [L18]
  <!-- SOURCE: Orwell's Rule #4, Strunk & White. "The dog bit the man" > "The man was bitten" -->

- **Vary sentence length significantly** [L19]
  <!-- "Perplexity" variation - AI detection marker. AI writes predictably. Humans mix it up. -->

- **Use contractions naturally** [L20]
  <!-- AI avoids contractions, sounding formal/robotic. "I'm writing" > "I am writing" -->

- **No em dashes; use commas or colons** [L21]
  <!-- Trevor Longino's framework. Em dashes interrupt flow. -->

- **Simple, direct language** [L22]
  <!-- Orwell's Rule #2: "Never use a long word where a short one will do" -->

- **Cut filler words, but don't make it robotic** [L23]
  <!-- Strunk & White: "Omit needless words" - but TOO polished = sounds like AI -->

## VOICE (Lines 27-32)

- **Write with confidence, but acknowledge uncertainty** [L27]
  <!-- AI writes with absolute certainty. "I think," "It seems" shows thoughtful analysis. -->

- **Professional but conversational** [L28]
  <!-- TEST: Would you say this out loud? If no, rewrite it. -->

- **Use "you" to address the reader** [L29]
  <!-- "You should consider..." (direct) > "One should consider..." (distant) -->

- **Clarity first, cleverness second** [L30]
  <!-- SOURCE: Strunk & White, Orwell. Don't sacrifice understanding for wit. -->

  <!-- AI produces generic "many experts believe." Human: "A 2024 Stanford study found..." -->

- **Show both upside AND potential risks** [L32]

## AVOID (Lines 44-49)

- **Hedge words that show weakness** [L44]
  <!-- "Perhaps we might consider trying" > "I recommend trying" -->

- **Empty praise without proof** [L45]
  <!-- SOURCE: Trevor Longino. "World-class" needs proof: "ranked #1 by Gartner" -->

- **Overused phrases and corporate jargon** [L46]
  <!-- "Synergy," "Circle back," "Move the needle" - TEST: Can a 12-year-old understand? -->

- **Passive voice (unless specifically needed)** [L47]
  <!-- Passive OK when actor unknown. Default to active for energy and clarity. -->

- **Starting every sentence the same way** [L48]
  <!-- BAD: "The system does X. The system enables Y." GOOD: "The system does X. It enables Y." -->

- **Generic, textbook-style explanations** [L49]

## SOURCES

**Classical Writing:**
- Strunk & White: "Elements of Style" - clarity, brevity, active voice
- George Orwell: "Politics and the English Language" - plain language, avoid jargon

**Anti-AI Detection (2025-2026):**
- Burstiness: Vary sentence/paragraph length
- Perplexity: Avoid predictable patterns
- Personality markers: Contractions, specific details, thoughtful uncertainty

**Professional Standards:**
- Trevor Longino's instant-marketing-mastery framework
- Journalism ethics - verify facts, cite sources
